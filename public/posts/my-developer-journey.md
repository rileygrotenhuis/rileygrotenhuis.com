### Simple Beginnings

I first dove into the world of programming my freshman year of high school back in 2015. That year my school started their new “Academic Academies” program that allowed students to explore a more specialized subject for their education that would gear them towards college and their professional career. Being the mindless teenager I was, I didn’t really put much thought into the academy I was choosing for myself and ended up picking computer science simply because I was good with computers and liked video games.

I took these computer science courses for the next few years but didn’t really find my true passion for programming until I decided to venture into self teaching myself outside of school. After falling down an internet search rabbit hole, I stumbled across Colt Steele’s web development bootcamp course on Udemy. His course was on a massive sale and decided to take some time during the summer to self teach myself everything I needed to learn about web development.

I learned the ins and outs of HTML, CSS and Javascript, Node, Express and the very basics of React. This knew found knowledge from the online bootcamp tied with my previous knowledge of Python from my high school courses, I had built myself a strong foundational knowledge in programming to begin my journey further down this rabbit hole.

### The Next Step

I decided to further my education in computer science at Missouri State University where I began my studies in the fall of 2019. During this time I built on top of my foundational programming knowledge and learned more in-depth topics such as C++, data structures, algorithms, relational databases and unit testing. For a brief period of time I also helped the computer science department as an undergraduate research assistant where I helped one of my professors in his study of artificial intelligence’s effect on cyber security.

In January of 2021, my sophomore year at MSU, I began my employment for the university’s Web Strategy & Development department as a Student Web Developer where I developed the internal tooling and web applications used by students, faculty and staff of the university. Some of my major contributions during my time there was upgrading the student employment department’s student worker screening and onboarding process software, as well as writing a tool that migrated thousands of web component building blocks from one content management system (CMS) to another.

### A New Opportunity

While working for the university, my plan was to continue my study of computer science - working as a Student Web Developer during the school year, and find internships at larger companies during the summer to build up my resume and portfolio for once I am done with schooling. During my time of internship hunting, I had submitted close to 150 application to various different companies across the country, completed dozens of first round coding interview problems, went through multiple rounds of interviews with various amounts of companies, all of which ended in some sort of rejection email or phone call.

During this time I had also worked on multiple side projects to build up my developer portfolio, including a personal branding website and a live public Spotify song request management tool. All of this work on personal projects as well as my branding on my LinkedIn profile caught the attention of a local recruiter in Springfield (where I reside) for the company I am currently employed for. He had asked if I was interested in completing a coding assessment and coming in for an interview, as they were looking for more junior level talent in the area.

A coding assessment and a few interviews later I was offered a full-time job with the company, to which I admitted to them I was hesitant at first since I was still finishing my degree at MSU. We came to an agreement for me to start part-time in November of 2021 and give it until the end of the year to reconsider the offer of coming on full-time. After only a single week of working for the company I realized that I had a massive opportunity here in my life and decided to take the leap of faith of putting a hold on my formal education and see what this career I’ve started can grow into.

### It All Payed Off

Which all leads us to today, where I am currently working as a software engineer for Midwestern Interactive working towards our mission of serving each other and serving our clients by providing an embedded teams solution to their business requirements. I am always working on personal projects outside of work to continue my growth and passion as an engineer and learn new skills in the technical area.
